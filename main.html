Chandratal


Chandratal, also known as the Moon Lake, is a stunning high-altitude lake located in the Lahaul and Spiti Valley of Himachal Pradesh, India. It sits at an altitude of 4,300 meters (14,100 feet) and is known for its crescent shape, crystal-clear blue waters, and breathtaking surroundings of snow-capped peaks.

  1. Origin of Name: "Chandra" means moon, and "Tal" means lake, giving it the name "Moon Lake."
  2. Geography: Situated near the Chandrabhaga mountain range, it is a source of the Chandra River, which later joins the Chenab River.
  3. Best Time to Visit: June to September, as the lake remains frozen during winters.
  4. Trekking Destination: A popular trek starts from Batal or Kunzum Pass (4,551m) and offers stunning landscapes.
  5. Mythological Significance: Believed to be the spot where Indra's chariot took Yudhishthira to heaven in the Mahabharata.
